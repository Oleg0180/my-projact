CALIBERS & OPTIONS

You pay only for the time spent at the range. Weapons, instructor services, scenarios are included!

45 ACP (AUTOMATIC COLT PISTOL)

The initial speed of a bullet can vary from 240 to 380 m / s, and energy from 500 to 780 J. One of the most important qualities of a personal short-barreled weapon is the stopping action of the bullet of the cartridge used in it, as well as the ability to make a series of accurate shots at maximum speed.

9X19MM (9X19 LUGER, 9MM NATO, 9X19 PARABELLUM, 9X19 PARA)

The history of Luger cartridges began in 1902, when the ammunition was developed by Georg Luger. The initial shape of the cartridges resembled a cone with a flat top. The cartridge is a full-time cartridge of NATO countries and is used in shooting trainings.

38 SP (SPECIAL)

A central ignition cartridge with a cylindrical sleeve having a protruding rim. Designed by the American company Smith & Wesson as a further development of the .38 Long Colt. It is widely used in revolvers nowadays; in addition, it is sometimes used in self-loading pistols and carbines.

40 SMITH & WESSON

A cartridge created for the US Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI). The cartridge looked very tempting - a combination of breakdown and stopping action of large calibers and relatively small recoil. And the cartridge itself is small in size. It is not only an police ammunition, but also a sports cartridge.
